FAQ

Any more questions? Contact us and we’ll be happy to help. We would love to hear from you.
Table of Contents

Infants

What languages do you teach?

We teach in French or English, we give bilingual lessons – Even though primarily, the native tongue of every Kinder Bubble child is English!

How do you encourage interactive play?

Cooperative play is something we truly value. But it has to be learned, as well as appeal to our social instincts… The best way to achieve this is by copying what we see. Therefore, our nursery teachers and educators model play and cooperate whenever they can, so the kids get a sense of what it looks like. Taking turns, acting gently, and speaking kindly: all these are examples of positive reinforcement. Soon enough, the play will start to diffuse social tension as a result.

How do you handle separation, transition and promotion?
We begin with short separation times, as children need to have faith that their caregiver will return. This builds trust and mutual understanding. Once these brief periods become the norm, we extend the length of separation. A comforter from home often helps, something like a blanket, toy or teddy bear. It’s normal for a child to cry during their initial state at kindergarten of semi-independence. Please remember that it’s a natural, healthy response to the love you’ve given them so far… For transitions and promotions, too, we create a plan that’s tailored to their personality traits.
When and where should I drop my child off for school?

You may drop your child off in the school driveway as early as 7:30 am, 20 minutes before school. Students meet and line up on the compound before school. Supervision is provided on the school compound every morning.

We recommend that parents walk their child or children to the school compound for at least the first few days of school. Park on the street or in the lot at 21 Quince Street and walk your child into the school compound playground.

Toddlers

What are your afternoon pick up procedures?

All children will be walked to the pick-up lines near the security post. Children will be supervised at all times. You may park your car on the street and walk to pick up your child.

What is the procedure for snacks and lunch?

Breakfast is served in the cafeteria every morning starting at 7:10 am. Children may bring a healthy snack to eat during morning recess, around 9:30 am. Kindergarten lunch time is 11:00 am. Students may purchase lunch from the cafeteria or bring a healthy lunch from home.

Where can I buy school uniforms?

Contact us at our reception desk for more info on purchasing school uniforms for your wards. Additional information will be updated on the website shortly.

What happens if my child gets sick or hurt?

Your child will be evaluated and treated immediately. You will be called right away if the injury is severe, or if your child needs to go home due to illness.

Preschool

What is some of the curriculum you use at the Preschool?

We use the Cambridge Primary Curriculum research-based, multi-sensory strategies to help children develop early learning skills, including phonics and literacy, handwriting, and everyday maths.

Does my child need to be potty-trained?
It is strongly encouraged that all children starting in Preschool, except for those entering the Toddler program, be potty-trained. However, it is not required.
Do children nap?

At Kinder Bubble, only children who stay for Creative Afternoons will have a rest period after lunch. At the preschool, students have a rest time after lunch, which often includes a read-aloud story time.

How do I find out what’s going on in my child’s class or in the larger school community?

Parents can access information instantly about their child(ren)’s classroom activities, schedule, and plans by calling which also provides access to family contact information and is where parents can update emergency contacts or the list of individuals allowed to pick up their child(ren). In addition to class-specific information, all school events, notices, weekly newsletters from classroom teachers, monthly newsletters from special teachers, Parent Association news and minutes, directory listings, school calendars, and photos live. In addition, we host numerous informational events for families throughout the year.
